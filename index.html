01 — About

From control theory to generative AI

I'm a machine learning engineer working at the intersection of generative AI and autonomous driving. At Torc Robotics, I build generative models and reinforcement-learning systems for scalable, closed-loop behavior simulation — the kind that lets self-driving trucks train and get evaluated against realistic, diverse traffic before ever touching a real highway.

What I care about most is closing the gap between research and what actually ships. In a fast-moving industry environment, that means taking ideas from papers and prototypes to production pipelines that a real engineering team can rely on — not just publishing a result, but making it fast, robust, and usable under real deadlines.

Before that, I spent three years at Honda Research Institute working on interaction-aware motion planning, multi-robot coordination, and human-AI teaming, and earned a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Delaware, where I developed decentralized control frameworks for connected and automated vehicles. That range — from optimal control theory to modern generative AI — is the throughline of my work: making autonomous systems that reason well about the other agents around them.
